How to Convert Hourly Pay to Salary (Step-by-Step Guide)

If you’re paid by the hour, it’s helpful to understand how your wage translates to an annual salary. Whether you’re budgeting for the year, comparing a job offer, or negotiating a raise, converting hourly to salary gives you a bigger picture of your income.

Hourly to Salary Formula

The basic formula to convert your hourly wage to an annual salary is:

Hourly Wage × Hours per Week × 52 Weeks per Year

Example: $20/hour × 40 hours/week × 52 weeks = $41,600 per year

Note: This assumes you work every week of the year. If you take unpaid time off or have seasonal work, your annual income will be lower.

Include Overtime, Bonuses, and PTO

  • Overtime: Add estimated overtime earnings to your annual pay.
  • Bonuses: Include recurring bonuses or incentives if applicable.
  • Paid Time Off (PTO): If your employer offers PTO, your annual pay stays the same even when you take time off.

Common Hourly to Salary Conversions

  • $15/hour ≈ $31,200 per year
  • $20/hour ≈ $41,600 per year
  • $25/hour ≈ $52,000 per year
  • $30/hour ≈ $62,400 per year
  • $50/hour ≈ $104,000 per year

Why Your Salary May Vary

Your exact yearly earnings depend on:

  • Actual hours worked per week
  • Unpaid time off or holidays
  • Overtime pay
  • Bonuses and commissions

Hourly to Salary Example Scenario

Alex works as a graphic designer making $28 per hour, working 38 hours per week. His estimated salary is:

$28 × 38 hours/week × 52 weeks = $55,328 per year

However, Alex usually takes 2 weeks of unpaid vacation, so his actual yearly pay is slightly lower.
