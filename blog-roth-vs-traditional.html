Roth vs Traditional IRA: Which One Should You Choose?

Planning for retirement? One of the biggest decisions is whether to invest in a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA. Both accounts help you grow your money tax-advantaged, but they work in opposite ways when it comes to taxes.

Key Differences Between Roth and Traditional IRAs

  • Roth IRA: Pay taxes on your contributions now, and enjoy tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
  • Traditional IRA: Contributions may be tax-deductible now, but withdrawals are taxed in retirement.

Contribution Limits for 2025

  • Under age 50: Up to $7,000 total contributions across all IRAs.
  • Age 50 or older: Up to $8,000 total (includes a $1,000 catch-up contribution).

These limits apply whether you choose Roth, Traditional, or a mix of both.

Income Limits for Roth IRA Contributions

High earners may be limited in how much they can contribute to a Roth IRA. For 2025:

  • Single filers: Contributions begin to phase out at $146,000 and end at $161,000 of modified adjusted gross income (MAGI).
  • Married filing jointly: Phase-out starts at $230,000 and ends at $240,000 of MAGI.

Withdrawal Rules

  • Roth IRA: No required minimum distributions (RMDs). Withdraw your contributions anytime tax- and penalty-free. Earnings can be withdrawn tax-free after age 59½ if the account has been open at least 5 years.
  • Traditional IRA: Required minimum distributions begin at age 73. Early withdrawals before age 59½ are taxed and penalized (with some exceptions).

Example Scenario

Mike is 30 years old and earns $60,000 per year. He expects his income and tax bracket to be higher in retirement. Mike chooses a Roth IRA to pay taxes now while his rates are lower and enjoy tax-free withdrawals later.

Sarah is 55 years old and earns $120,000. She wants to lower her taxable income today and expects her retirement income to be lower. Sarah chooses a Traditional IRA for the current tax break.

Pros and Cons Summary

✅ Roth IRA Pros:

  • Tax-free withdrawals in retirement
  • No RMDs
  • Best for younger earners or those expecting higher future tax rates

⚠️ Roth IRA Cons:

  • No tax deduction upfront
  • Income limits restrict eligibility for some earners

✅ Traditional IRA Pros:

  • Potential tax deduction today
  • No income limits for contributions (but limits apply for deductions if you have a workplace plan)

⚠️ Traditional IRA Cons:

  • Withdrawals are taxed in retirement
  • RMDs are required starting at age 73
