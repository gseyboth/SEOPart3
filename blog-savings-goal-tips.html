How to Set a Savings Goal (and Actually Reach It)

Whether you're saving for an emergency fund, a vacation, or a new car, having a clear savings goal can help you stay motivated and on track. But it’s not enough to just say, “I want to save more.” A strong savings goal includes a target amount and a realistic timeline.

Step 1: Set a Specific Goal Amount

Decide what you're saving for and how much you’ll need. Example goals:

  • $1,000 for an emergency fund
  • $5,000 for a vacation
  • $15,000 for a car down payment

Step 2: Choose a Deadline

Pick a target date for your savings goal. The shorter your timeline, the more you'll need to save each month.

Step 3: Calculate Your Monthly Savings

Divide your goal amount by the number of months until your deadline. For example, saving $5,000 in 10 months means saving $500 per month.

Example Scenario

Jessica wants to save $3,600 for a vacation 12 months from now. She’ll need to save:

$3,600 ÷ 12 = $300 per month

Step 4: Automate Your Savings

Consider setting up an automatic transfer to your savings account right after you get paid. This helps make saving a habit.
